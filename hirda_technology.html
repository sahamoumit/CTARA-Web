Hirda Decortication Machine

The objective of this intervention was to design a decortication machine that can be used for decortication of both Hirda

Enhancing Tribal Livelihoods Through Fruit Decortication

Collaboration with NGO Shashwat

This project was undertaken in partnership with NGO Shashwat, which works with tribal communities in Maharashtra. Trees of Hirda (also called Harad or Hartaki) and Behada grow abundantly in the forests near Bhimashankar, where the NGO operates. These fruits, known for their medicinal properties, are in high demand for Ayurvedic products like Triphala Churna. Tribals collect these fruits from the jungle and manually decorticate the sun-dried fruits using stones, a labor-intensive and exhausting process.

Addressing Drudgery and Boosting Income

RuTAG IIT Bombay initiated this project to tackle the challenges faced by tribals in decorticating Hirda fruits. The aim was to reduce the physical strain involved in this activity while enhancing the market value of decorticated fruits. By addressing these issues, the project also sought to create better livelihood opportunities for the tribal communities.

Salient features: What Sets It Apart
  • Easy to handle and operate
  • Designed for safe operation
  • Use of single phase motor (makes it convenient to use at any place)
  • Suitable for decortication of various sizes of Hirda
  • Suitable for decortication of medicinal fruits such as Hirda.

Adoption & Impact

RuTAG designed a prototype, which was refined and modified multiple-times based on feedback from end-users during field trials. At present, it is being used by SHGs based at Ambegaon block and Velhe block of Pune district. The training for operating the machine was provided to the SHG members and they are using it from last two years for Hirda decortication.Recently, one unit has also been disseminated at Hoshangabad district of Madhya Pradesh. The feedback from end users has been quite positive.

Tribal Development department of Government of Maharashtra funded a project for dissemination of 12 Hirda decortication m achines in Ambegaon, Junnar Block in 2020-21. During this period, a farmer producer company (FPC) was formed in Ambegaon block to expand decorticated Hirda business and establish direct linkage with wholesale traders, Ayurveda drug manufacturing companies.

The SHG women are now able to get price of Rs. 50 per kilogram for decorticated Hirda as compared to Rs. 15 per kilogram for non- decorticated Hirda. They are selling decorticated Hirda to vendors dealing in Ayurvedic medicines. (Based at Pune and New Bombay).

RuTAG IITB Intervention
  • RuTAG IIT Bombay developed two machines: the Hirda Decortication Machine and the Hirda Grader Machine
  • The Hirda Decortication Machine can process 50 kg of Hirda per hour
  • The Hirda Grader Machine can grade 100 kg of Hirda per hour
