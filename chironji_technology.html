Multi-purpose Processor

PI: Prof. Amit Arora / Prof. Vishal Sardeshpande ,C-TARA

The goal of this intervention is to design a user-friendly "Chironji Decortication and Multipurpose Processing Machine" that minimizes damage to the Chironji kernels while also efficiently processing dal and flour, ensuring a high-quality output with minimal wastage.

Need for a Multi-Purpose Processing Solution

Challenges Faced by Rural Communities

In rural areas, there is a growing need for a versatile processing machine that can decorticate Chironji seeds as well as process grains and pulses. The lack of such machines limits the potential for Self Help Groups (SHGs) and Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs) to set up small-scale, sustainable processing units in villages.

Impact on Local Economy and Employment

Without access to multi-purpose equipment, rural communities face difficulties in processing Chironji and other grains. This results in labor-intensive work that requires specialized machinery, limiting local economic growth. A machine that can handle both tasks efficiently would improve productivity, create job opportunities, and help boost the local economy.

Salient features: What Sets It Apart
  • Chironji decorticate: 40kg/hr
  • Milling of pulses : 40kg/hr
  • Flour making from cereals: 100kg/hr
  • Can be operated with single phase electricity connection
  • Power requirement: 5 hp motor

RuTAG IITB Intervention

RuTAG at IIT Bombay has designed a ‘Chironji Decortication cum Multi purpose processor Machine’ for efficiently decorticating Chironji nuts to obtain Chironji kernels (seeds) which can be sold in the market for higher price also making dal and flour .

Adoption & Impact
  • MPP technology is adopted in Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, and Jharkhand, covering seven districts
  • It enables year-round processing of dal, wheat, and millets, empowering rural communities economically
  • The MPP reduces payback periods from 2-3 years to 1 year, ensuring continuous income
  • It boosts agricultural productivity and food security by improving processing efficiency
  • The Chironji and dal processing market in India is valued at ₹505 to ₹710 crore
  • Madhya Pradesh alone has a ₹1.5-2 crore opportunity by capturing 1% of the dal processing market
