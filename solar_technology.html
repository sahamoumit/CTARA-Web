Solar Food Cart

The intervention aims to provide a sustainable and cost-effective energy solution for street food vendors by utilizing renewable solar energy. The goal is to reduce fuel costs, enhance food safety, and promote an environmentally friendly alternative to traditional cooking methods

Challenges Faced by Street Food Vendors

High Operating Costs and Environmental Impact

Street food vendors often depend on expensive and polluting fuels like LPG or kerosene for their cooking needs. This reliance significantly increases their operating costs, making it harder for them to sustain their businesses while maintaining profitability.

Limited Energy Access and Food Safety Concerns

The lack of reliable energy sources affects the quality and safety of food preparation for street vendors. This not only compromises the hygiene of their offerings but also contributes to environmental pollution, posing challenges to both vendors and the communities they serve.

Salient features: What Sets It Apart
  • Solar panels: 1340 Wp [400 Wp (x2), 270 Wp (x2)]
  • Operating time for 1200 W induction cook stove: 4-6 hours
  • Provision for charge controller along with the battery
  • Green technology with low carbon footprint

RuTAG IITB Intervention

Solar-Powered and Mobile Cooking Solution

The Solar Food Cart is a lightweight, mobile unit equipped with solar panels and batteries, providing a clean and efficient energy source. This solar-powered setup ensures that street food vendors can cook without relying on polluting fuels, offering a sustainable solution.

Uninterrupted Operations and Durability

Designed for easy transport, the Solar Food Cart includes integrated charge controllers and durable batteries, ensuring reliable and continuous cooking operations for vendors. The system offers a noise-free and low-maintenance alternative, making it ideal for street food vendors in need of a reliable cooking solution.

Adoption & Impact
  • The Solar Food Cart has been introduced in Maharashtra, with three units currently in use
  • It helps reduce fuel expenses and lowers carbon emissions, promoting environmental sustainability
  • The cart enhances food safety and hygiene, improving overall cooking conditions for vendors
  • This solution contributes to the vendors' profitability by offering better cooking facilities
  • The market for solar food carts is expected to grow from INR 200-250 crore to INR 400-450 crore by 2028
  • Rural expansion potential is strong, fueled by government incentives and rising fuel costs
