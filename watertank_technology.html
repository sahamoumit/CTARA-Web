Natural fiber based surface water tanks

PI: Prof. T.I Eldho, Civil Engineering
Inventor: Mr. Ulhas Paranjape, Jalvardhini Prathisthan

The project focused on testing the effectiveness of surface water tanks made from locally available natural fibers. The goal was to evaluate how well these tanks could prevent seepage and withstand long-term use, offering a sustainable and durable solution for water storage.

Sustainable Water Storage Solutions

Evaluating Natural Fiber Surface Water Tanks

This project explored the potential of surface water tanks constructed from locally available natural fibers. By using materials readily accessible in the region, these tanks were designed to offer an eco-friendly and cost-effective option for water storage. The focus was to test their practicality and performance under real-world conditions.

Assessing Seepage and Durability

The key objective of the study was to measure how well these tanks could minimize water seepage while maintaining their structural integrity over time. By ensuring both effectiveness and durability, the project aimed to develop a reliable and sustainable solution for communities in need of efficient water storage systems.

Technology for Construction of Water Tanks using Natural Fibers

NGO ‘Jalvardhini’ has come up with a novel technique for water tank construction using natural fibres such as coconut, banana and flax. While in an RCC tank steel is used as reinforcement, in a natural fibre based tank, the fibres are used as a substitute for steel to cope with tensile stress and to deal with shrinkage. The construction costs of these tanks are quite low (approximately Rs. 2 to 3/ litre capacity) in comparison with RCC. Though this technology had been in use for 4-5 years, the ability of the natural fibre water tanks to deal with seepage and their durability had not been evaluated scientifically. NGO Jalvardhini approached RuTAG IITB with this issue.

Salient features: What Sets It Apart
  • Easy to construct-local mason can be trained in 2-3 days
  • Green technology - use of natural fibers for construction of water tank
  • Technology useful for rain water harvesting
  • Low cost-cost of construction is 1/3rd of RCC construction

RuTAG IITB Intervention

Construction and Testing of Fiber-Based Tanks

Eight tanks made from different natural fibers (coconut, banana, and flax) were built at IIT Bombay’s campus at a 1:10 scale for scientific study. These tanks were designed to mimic the conditions of actual field tanks used by the NGO. Each tank was equipped with a hook gauge to monitor seepage at regular intervals, and the cracking, durability, and strength were closely monitored.

Performance and Strength Evaluation

The tanks showed minimal seepage and evaporation loss, making them effective for water storage. Slabs made from different fibers and cement were tested for flexural strength after curing for 28 days. Medium-density coconut fiber (300 to 400 gms/sq.mt) and flax and banana fibers (300 gms/sq.mt) were found to offer the best strength and durability.

Cost-Effectiveness and Environmental Benefits

These fiber-based tanks require very little maintenance and offer a cost-effective solution for water storage. Made from natural fibers, they are an environmentally friendly "green technology." The cost of these tanks was significantly lower, around Rs. 2-3 per liter of capacity, compared to more than Rs. 10 for RCC tanks, making them a sustainable and affordable alternative.
