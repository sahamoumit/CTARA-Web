RuTAG Outreach and Training Programs

Event Date: December 25, 2024

Location: Kolhapur District

Description: RuTAG visited five vendors in the agro-tech sector to explore innovations. These vendors are actively working on technologies that align with RuTAG’s mission of sustainable rural development.

Event Date: December 24, 2024

Location: Kolhapur

Description: The meeting, chaired by Dr. Suhas Patil, focused on agricultural innovations by students and professors. RuTAG shared its work in rural areas and explored potential collaborations with the university.

Event Date: December 17-19, 2024

Location: IIT Bombay

Description: RuTAG participated in this prominent technical event, showcasing the Solar Hydro Distiller and other technologies. Students engaged with RuTAG representatives, gaining insights into their work and contributions to rural technology.

Event Date: December 11-15, 2024

Location: Moshi, Pune

Description: Kisan Krishi Fest, one of Maharashtra’s largest agricultural events, attracted 1.5 lakh daily visitors. RuTAG presented technologies like the Solar Hydro Distiller and farm equipment (e.g., Hirada Dicroticator, Cow Lifts). Farmers expressed interest, with inquiries about the Solar Hydro Distiller and Cow Lifts.

Event Date: December 10, 2024

Location: Kharghar, Navi Mumbai

Description: Doctors and nurses received training on digital stethoscopes. The Ayusink team conducted live demonstrations, showcasing the device’s application on over 20 patients. This session highlighted the technology’s potential for healthcare in rural settings.

Event Date: December 5-7, 2024

Location: Srinagar, Kashmir

Description: The meeting, chaired by the Principal Scientific Adviser to GOI, Prof. Sood, discussed expanding vendors for the Multipurpose Processor and scaling up the Hydro Distiller for medical applications. Key points included addressing rural demand for solar charge controllers, identifying end users, and developing low-end technologies for Parkinson’s disease.

Event Date: November 27, 2024

Location: C - TARA

Description: The PAC meeting reported significant progress on RuTAG-supported technologies like the Subji Cooler, MarketMirchi.com, and water shafts for small community water supplies. The Subji Cooler transitioned to an EMI/rental model with over 1,000 units disseminated. New project proposals were approved, including improvements to digital stethoscopes, cost-effective methods for detecting sickle cell anemia, and nutritional interventions for pediatric oncology.

Event Date: November 14, 2024

Stakeholder: Ms. Syrpailin K. Hujon

Description: A vegetable vendor using the Sabjee Cooler shared insights on its effectiveness. She stores up to 30 kg of vegetables for 3-4 days, demonstrating the cooler’s utility in rural areas. This visit provided valuable feedback on the technology’s practical application.

Event Date: November 14, 2024

Stakeholder: Mr. Welstar Blah

Description: A farmer shared challenges related to water scarcity during the flowering stage of crops, as springs often dry up. A cement structure was built around the spring to capture water, but it dries out completely from January to March. A solution was proposed to install a 20,000-liter water tank for irrigation. The farmer’s land consists of mixed clay soil and some soft rock, with sufficient labor availability.

Event Date: November 13, 2024

Location: Shillong, Meghalaya

Description: Dr. Collin Renthalie, Technical Advisor at NECTAR, discussed the scope for small-scale technologies in the Northeast. Technologies presented included water shafts, hydro distillers, water tanks, and bamboo playhouses for saffron cultivation. The promotion of Sabjee Coolers in 25 clusters and potential applications of pineapple fibers were also explored.

Event Date: November 13, 2024

Location: Shillong, Meghalaya

Description: The training session was attended by over 40 participants, including Smt. Lyngwa, MCS (Commissioner for Persons with Disabilities), and Ms. Bertha G. Dkhar, ED of Bethany Society. The session introduced digital stethoscopes and diabetic foot scanners. Dr. Rupali Khanolkar emphasized the importance of medical devices in improving rural livelihoods, while Dr. Lyngwa highlighted the need for data and technology adoption in Meghalaya.

Event Date: November 12, 2024

Location: IIT Guwahati

Description: The visit focused on collaboration for bamboo and e-bicycles. Discussions with Prof. Kakoti and Mr. Nilkamal suggested converting bamboo bicycles with balancing issues into solar tricycles equipped with baskets. Additional discussions explored the pros and cons of various designs, including tricycles and bicycles with or without balancing wheels. RuTAG IIT Bombay agreed to share its bamboo bicycle design for further development.

Event Date: October 1-4, 2024

Location: IIT Bombay

Description: This workshop aimed to address the current and future needs of empowering rural communities. The discussions focused on formulating academic and R&D programs to address rural challenges. RuTAG showcased various technologies and highlighted ongoing dissemination efforts in rural communities.
