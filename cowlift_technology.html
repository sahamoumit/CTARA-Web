Assistive Device for Ailing Cattle

PI: Prof. Tanmay Bhandakkar, Mechanical Engineering/ Prof. Vishal Sardeshpande, C-TARA

The aim is to create a portable cow lift to help a downer cow, weighing up to 500 kg, stand up for medical treatment. The lift was designed to be lightweight and easy to transport, ensuring quick assistance for the cow's recovery

Addressing Downer Cow Challenge : A call for innovation

Downer Cow Syndrome and Its Impact

Downer Cow Syndrome affects cows both before and after calving, causing severe weakness in their leg muscles. This condition makes it difficult for affected cows to stand or walk on their own, and if left untreated, it can result in death. Timely medical intervention is critical to improving the chances of recovery.

Request for a Portable Cow Lift

An NGO approached RuTAG IIT Bombay, seeking help to address this issue. They explained that existing cow lifts in government veterinary institutes are too bulky to transport to fields or cowsheds, where the cows often need treatment. To solve this problem, they requested the design of a portable cow lift that could easily support downer cows and assist in providing timely medical care.

Salient features: What Sets It Apart
  • The animal can be easily lifted by just 1-2 people
  • Different components of the structure can be easily assembled at site where it is needed
  • The supporting structure is designed to give maximum comfort to the animal
  • The instrument has been designed after taking into account anatomy of the animal

RuTAG IITB Intervention

Initial Research and Prototyping

We began our project by studying the body dimensions of various cow breeds in collaboration with the Bombay Veterinary College, Parel. This research was essential to ensure that our design would accommodate different sizes and types of cows. We also examined the animal lift currently used at the institute to gather valuable insights and inspiration for our prototype. Our first prototype was modeled after this existing design, but it revealed several limitations that impeded its overall effectiveness in lifting downer cows.

Design Iteration and Feedback

Recognizing the shortcomings of our initial prototype, we proceeded to design a second version, incorporating feedback from the NGO and conducting field trials. This iterative process allowed us to refine the design further and ensure it met the practical needs of the animals and caregivers. The second prototype focused on addressing the issues identified during the testing phase, resulting in a more functional and user-friendly solution.

Final Design and Implementation

The final prototype features a superstructure made of MS hollow square pipes, providing strength and stability. A chain pulley is mounted on top to serve as the lifting mechanism, while a canvas support is positioned under the cow's belly, securely attached to the chain pulley through a horizontal framework. After conducting successful trials and implementing minor modifications based on observations, we finalized the design, ensuring it effectively supports downer cows and facilitates their medical treatment.

Adoption & Impact
  • Successfully adopted in nine districts across Maharashtra, Karnataka, Madhya Pradesh, and Chhattisgarh
  • Improves animal welfare, enhancing recovery rates and reducing suffering
  • Provides economic benefits to farmers by lowering treatment costs and boosting productivity
  • Market potential to impact 1.5 to 3 million cattle, with annual revenue potential of ₹1.5 billion to ₹3 billion
  • Growth opportunities through partnerships with NGOs, government bodies, and agricultural cooperatives
