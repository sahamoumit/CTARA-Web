Mobile Subjee Cooler

PI: Prof. Satish Agnihotri, C-TARA
Inventor: Mr. Vikash Kumar Jha, Ms. Sharayu Kulkarni, Mr. Gunavant Nehete, C-TARA, IIT Bombay

The aim of this project was to create a low-cost cold storage system for marginal farmers. Called the Subjee Cooler, this technology helps farmers keep their produce fresh for longer, reducing waste and improving their income.

Rising Agrarian Crisis: Landholding and Investment Challenges

Challenges Faced by Farmers in India

The agrarian crisis in India is worsening due to factors such as smaller landholdings, high input costs, significant post-harvest losses on farms, and a rapidly depleting water table. These issues have made farming increasingly difficult, especially for small-scale farmers who struggle to sustain their livelihoods.

Landholding Trends and Investment Barriers

According to the NSSO 70th round (January–December 2013), marginal and small farmers make up about 85% of the farming population and own nearly 53% of agricultural land. While the number of marginal landholders has increased, landholdings in other categories have declined. These small-scale farmers often lack the resources to invest in expensive technologies like motorized pumps, cold storage systems, tractors, and harvesters, limiting their ability to improve productivity and reduce losses.

Salient features: What Sets It Apart
  • Keeps fruits, vegetables fresh for 3-4 days
  • Requires no external energy for operation
  • Sustainable solution for reducing post-harvest losses

RuTAG IITB Intervention

Designed for Small Farmers and Efficient Storage

The Subjee-Cooler is ideal for farmers with plots or orchards smaller than 0.5 acres. It is designed to preserve vegetables, flowers, and fruits for 4–5 days without requiring any external energy source. The cooler operates efficiently with just 20 liters of water daily, making it a cost-effective and sustainable solution for small-scale farmers.

Multi-Purpose Storage and Ripening Benefits

In addition to keeping produce fresh, the Subjee-Cooler serves as a natural ripening chamber for fruits. It helps maintain the freshness, color, and visual appeal of stored items, enabling farmers to harvest only as needed. This feature prevents losses caused by under- or over-mature produce, offering flexibility and control to farmers during the harvest process.

Economic Advantages Through Reduced Wastage

The Subjee-Cooler allows farmers to store unsold produce for a few days, enhancing their bargaining power and preventing distress sales. By reducing wastage, this technology not only helps farmers secure better prices but also increases income for farmer-traders and retailers. With a storage capacity of up to 100 kg of leafy vegetables or 125 kg of other green vegetables, the Subjee-Cooler offers a practical and scalable solution to improve livelihoods and reduce losses.

Adoption & Impact
  • RuKart has installed over 450 Subjee Coolers in states like Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh, Gujarat, Meghalaya, Odisha, and Tamil Nadu
  • These coolers help reduce distress sales for farmers growing vegetables, flowers, and fruits
  • A report by the M.S. Swaminathan Foundation in Odisha found that farmers using the Subjee Cooler saw over a 30% increase in the selling price of their produce
  • Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs), like Haritha Mitra in Eluru, Andhra Pradesh, use the Subjee Cooler to store produce at their shops
  • Retailers also use the Subjee Cooler to store flowers, fruits, and vegetables
