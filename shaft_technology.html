Hydraulic Isolation Structure (shaft) for Water Supply Schemes

PI: Prof. Pradip Kalbar, Centre for Urban Science and Engineering (CUSE)
Inventor: Dr. Anuj Ghorpade, CUSE, IIT Bombay

The intervention seeks to achieve equitable water distribution in rural areas, improving access for all households and increasing revenue generation for local water supply schemes

Challenges of Unequal Water Distribution in Rural Areas

Inefficiencies in Conventional Water Supply Systems

Conventional rural water supply systems often face the challenge of unequal water distribution. This issue arises due to varying water pressure across different regions, which results in some areas receiving excess water while others face shortages. This uneven distribution leads to wastage and inefficiencies in water management.

Impact on Consumers and Revenue

Unequal water supply causes dissatisfaction among consumers as some households or regions receive inadequate water. Additionally, this imbalance contributes to revenue losses for service providers, as dissatisfied consumers may withhold payment or demand refunds. Addressing this challenge is crucial for ensuring equitable water access and improving system efficiency.

Salient features: What Sets It Apart
  • Hydraulic Grade Line (HGL) adapts to demand changes
  • Open-to-sky design for entrapped air removal
  • Simultaneous supply to multiple zones
  • Minimal moving parts for easy maintenance
  • Cost-effective compared to ESRs

RuTAG IITB Intervention

Ensuring Equal Water Distribution

The hydraulic isolation structure is a breakthrough technology designed to resolve pressure imbalances in rural water supply systems. By maintaining consistent pressure across outlets, it ensures equal water distribution, addressing inefficiencies and improving access for all households.

Cost-Effective and Efficient Solution

This system supports multiple outlets for simultaneous water supply, simplifying operations and reducing complexity. It is also a cost-effective alternative to Elevated Storage Reservoirs (ESRs), making it a practical solution for widespread adoption in rural areas.

Adoption & Impact
  • Implemented in Maharashtra and West Bengal, with five units currently in operation
  • Ensures equitable water distribution for rural households in India
  • Enhances consumer satisfaction and improves revenue collection for service providers
  • Aligns with government programs like the Jal Jeevan Mission
  • Holds significant potential for improving water management in over 600,000 rural villages
