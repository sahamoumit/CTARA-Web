Climate-Agnostic Onion Storage Structuures

PI: Prof. Amit Arora ,C-TARA
Inventor: Mr. Nilesh Vadgave, C-TARA, IIT Bombay

The intervention seeks to minimize post-harvest losses by 20 to 40% through improved curing and storage conditions. It also aims to stabilize farmer incomes by enabling longer storage durations and better market access.

Challenges in India's Onion Storage Sector

Post-Harvest Losses in the Onion Sector

India’s onion industry faces huge post-harvest losses, amounting to nearly ₹10,000 crores every year. A significant portion of the produce is wasted due to improper storage conditions and handling practices.

Impact on Farmers and Quality

Traditional storage methods are inefficient, causing onions to spoil quickly. This leads to a drop in quality, financial losses for farmers, and limited availability of fresh onions in the market.

Salient features: What Sets It Apart
  • Scale-up system for integrated curing and storage of 20 tonnes
  • Reduces storage losses by 50%
  • Enables farmers to process onions with an inbuilt drying chamber

RuTAG IITB Intervention

Efficient Storage for Large Quantities

The Integrated Curing and Storage system is a climate-independent solution capable of storing up to 20 tonnes of onions. By combining advanced curing methods with optimized storage conditions, it helps reduce post-harvest losses and preserves the quality of onions for longer periods.

Added Value Through Onion Processing

This innovative system features an inbuilt drying chamber, enabling farmers to dry and process onions. This added functionality provides an opportunity to explore value-added products, increasing income potential for farmers.

Adoption & Impact
  • Features an inbuilt drying chamber for onion processing
  • Enables farmers to produce value-added onion products
  • Opens new opportunities for increasing income
  • Supports diversification in onion-based farming practices
